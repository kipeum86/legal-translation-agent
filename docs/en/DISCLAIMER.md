## Not Legal Advice

Nothing in this repository — including translations, terminology mappings, glossary entries, structural verification reports, or any other output generated by the agent — constitutes legal advice. The outputs are **informational and assistive only**. Do not rely on them as a substitute for qualified legal counsel or professional human translation.

Legal translation requires jurisdiction-specific expertise that goes beyond linguistic accuracy. Terminology conventions differ between legal systems (e.g., BGB vs. UCC vs. PRC Civil Code), and a single mistranslated term can change the legal effect of a provision. **Only a qualified legal professional and/or certified translator familiar with both the source and target legal systems can ensure a translation is fit for its intended legal purpose.**

## Not a Certified Translation

Outputs from this agent are **machine-assisted translations**. They do not constitute certified, sworn, or notarized translations. They are not suitable for:

- Court filings or litigation without professional review and certification
- Regulatory submissions that require certified translations
- Notarized documents or apostille procedures
- Any context where a certified human translator's seal or signature is required

## AI Limitations — Hallucinations and Errors

This project is powered by a large language model (Claude). LLMs can and do produce **hallucinations, factual errors, and incorrect translations**. Outputs may:

- Mistranslate legal terms, altering the legal effect of a clause or provision
- Omit sentences, sub-clauses, or defined terms despite the zero-omission design goal
- Apply terminology conventions from the wrong jurisdiction (e.g., PRC terms in a Taiwan context)
- Produce "translationese" — grammatically correct but unnatural phrasing that a native legal reader would flag
- Generate inconsistent terminology despite glossary enforcement
- Fabricate or misattribute legal terminology that does not exist in the target legal system

While this agent includes built-in safeguards — **dual-pass translation, comparative synthesis, structural verification, and back-translation checking (Hard mode)** — these safeguards reduce, not eliminate, the risk of error. **A qualified human must always review and verify every translation** before any reliance or action.

## Glossary and Library Accuracy

The agent maintains persistent glossaries and supports user-managed reference libraries. Important caveats:

- **Glossary accumulation**: The glossary grows automatically with each translation job. Incorrect term mappings introduced early may propagate to future translations unless manually corrected
- **Library references**: Gold-standard reference translations in the Library are user-supplied. The agent does not verify their accuracy or currency
- **Library ingest**: The `/library ingest` command moves files from the inbox to the Library's reference structure. Files are organized by language pair and role (source/target), but the agent relies on user input for correct classification
- **Format conversion**: Reference files are parsed via automated pipelines (python-docx, pymupdf, MarkItDown). Conversion may introduce formatting errors or lose nuanced content (tables, footnotes, complex layouts)

## Data Security and Privacy

All processing runs **locally on your filesystem** — no external servers or databases are involved. However, the agent makes LLM API calls, which means **document text and your instructions are sent to the model provider** (Anthropic).

If you intend to use this for real legal work with confidential documents:

- **At minimum**, use a **commercial API key** (not a free-tier or consumer plan) so that standard commercial data handling terms apply.
- **Ideally**, use an **Enterprise plan with a Zero Data Retention (ZDR) agreement**. Note that ZDR is **not a complete guarantee** — your data still passes through the provider's infrastructure. Review Anthropic's official documentation on [ZDR and data handling](https://code.claude.com/docs/en/zero-data-retention) to determine whether it meets your organization's requirements.
- **Always** check with your organization's **security, compliance, and IT departments** before processing any confidential, privileged, or personally identifiable information through any AI tool.
