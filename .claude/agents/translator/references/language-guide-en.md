# English Legal Translation Guide

## Register & Formality

Formal legal prose. Three jurisdiction variants:

| Variant | Citation System | Key Markers |
|---|---|---|
| **US** | Bluebook | Articles/Sections, Exhibits, "shall"→"must" trend |
| **UK** | OSCOLA | Clauses, Schedules, no full stops in abbreviations |
| **International** | Neutral | Avoid jurisdiction-specific idioms |

Infer variant from source document or ask user.

---

## Article Numbering Convention

### US Style
```
Article I (Roman numerals for top level)
  Section 1.1
    (a)
      (i)
        (A)
```

### UK Style
```
Clause 1 (Arabic numerals throughout)
  1.1
    (a)
      (i)
```

**Key difference**: US uses "Articles" and "Sections" in contracts; UK uses "Clauses" (reserves "Sections" for statutes).

### Attachments
| US | UK |
|---|---|
| Exhibits | Schedules |
| Appendices | Appendices / Annexes |

## Defined Term Convention

### At Definition
```
"Software" means the computer program described in Exhibit A.
```
Or parenthetical: `XYZ Corporation (the "Licensor")`

### Subsequent Uses
- Always capitalized: `the Software`, `the Licensee`
- No quotation marks after first definition
- Must be used consistently — never vary the term

### Cross-References
- Modern preferred: lowercase `section 5.4`, `exhibit A`
- Some firms: `Section 5.4` (capitalized)
- Always include specific reference, never vague "herein"

## Emphasis Convention

| Format | Usage |
|---|---|
| **Bold** | Headings, section titles |
| ALL CAPS | WARRANTY DISCLAIMERS, LIMITATION OF LIABILITY, JURY WAIVER |
| *Italics* | Case names, Latin terms, emphasis |
| "Quotes" | Defined terms at first introduction only |

### ALL CAPS — Legal Basis
- **UCC § 2-316**: Warranty disclaimers must be "conspicuous"
- ALL CAPS is one accepted method (not the only one)
- Sections commonly in ALL CAPS: WARRANTY DISCLAIMER, LIMITATION OF LIABILITY, INDEMNIFICATION, ARBITRATION

### When Translating FROM English ALL CAPS
Map to target language equivalent:
- KO/ZH/JA: Bold (these languages have no case)
- EN→EN: Preserve ALL CAPS

## Legal Citation Format

### Bluebook (US)
| Type | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Federal statute | `Title U.S.C. § Section` | `42 U.S.C. § 1983` |
| Case | `Party v. Party, Vol. Reporter Page (Court Year)` | `Burwell v. Hobby Lobby, 573 U.S. 682 (2014)` |
| Regulation | `Title C.F.R. § Section` | `21 C.F.R. § 312.32` |

### OSCOLA (UK)
| Type | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | `Name of Act Year, s N` | `Human Rights Act 1998, s 6` |
| Case (neutral) | `Party v Party [Year] Court Number` | `Corr v IBC Vehicles [2008] UKHL 13` |
| Case (pre-2001) | `Party v Party [Year] Report Page` | `Donoghue v Stevenson [1932] AC 562` |

## Date Format

| Variant | Format | Example |
|---|---|---|
| US | Month DD, YYYY | March 16, 2026 |
| UK | DD Month YYYY | 16 March 2026 |
| International (ISO) | YYYY-MM-DD | 2026-03-16 |

**Never use all-numeric dates** (3/16/2026 vs 16/3/2026) in international documents.

## Key Legal Terminology

### "Shall" vs "Must" vs "Will"
| Term | Meaning | Modern Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| **shall** | Imposes obligation | Avoid — ambiguous; courts have interpreted it multiple ways |
| **must** | Mandatory requirement | Preferred by plain-language advocates |
| **will** | Future tense / obligation | Plain-language alternative to "shall" |
| **may** | Permission / discretion | Universally accepted |

**Rule**: Never mix "shall," "must," and "will" with the same intended meaning in one document.

### Archaic Terms to Avoid (Modern Alternatives)
| Archaic | Modern |
|---|---|
| herein | "in this agreement" / "in this section" |
| hereinafter | Simply parenthetical: `("SNB")` |
| notwithstanding | "although" / "despite" / "even if" |
| whereas | "Background:" heading |
| witnesseth | Delete |
| aforesaid | Specific cross-reference |

### Recitals
- US: "WHEREAS... NOW, THEREFORE..."
- UK/Modern: "Background" or "Recitals" heading
- **Rule**: Recitals must NEVER contain obligations, warranties, or duties

## US vs UK Key Differences

### Spelling
| US | UK |
|---|---|
| license (noun/verb) | licence (noun), license (verb) |
| defense | defence |
| judgment (legal) | judgment (courts also use this) |
| authorize | authorise |

### Terminology
| Concept | US | UK |
|---|---|---|
| Attachments | Exhibits | Schedules |
| Subdivisions | Articles/Sections | Clauses |
| Legal entity | Corporation | Company (Ltd, PLC) |
| Lawyer | Attorney | Solicitor / Barrister |

## Common Document Structures

### EULA
1. Definitions → 2. License Grant → 3. IP Ownership → 4. Restrictions → 5. User Obligations → 6. Privacy → 7. Third-Party Components → 8. WARRANTY DISCLAIMER → 9. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY → 10. Indemnification → 11. Term/Termination → 12. Governing Law → 13. General Provisions

### NDA
1. Definitions → 2. Scope → 3. Obligations → 4. Exclusions → 5. Term → 6. Return/Destruction → 7. Remedies → 8. No License → 9. Governing Law → 10. General Provisions
